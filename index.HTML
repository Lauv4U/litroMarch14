Monetization

How Litro earns.

Not from users, from the ecosystem. The only direct user revenue is the Master subscription — everything else comes from the outside.

Phase 1 — Launch with MVP
🖨️ Hardware bundles + consumables

Pre-configured tablet + printer + cash drawer kit. Ongoing: receipt paper rolls, accessories. Fear of setup is the product.

Phase 1 — Launch with MVP
🔗 Supplier affiliate redirects

Low stock alert → "Reorder" button → affiliate link to supplier e-commerce. Listing fees + per-order commission. Supplier pays, not the store.

Phase 2
🏦 Embedded finance — micro-loans

Partner with Tonik, CIMB, or Blend PH. Store's sales data = pre-approved loan offer in-app. Referral fee per origination. No lending risk.

Phase 2 — Needs receipt volume
🧾 Receipt advertising

Blank receipt footer = geo-targeted ad space. FMCG brands pay CPM to appear on receipts printed in their target areas.

Phase 2–3
🏷️ White-label for distributors

Distributor pays ₱5–20k/mo for a branded Litro for their store network. One deal = hundreds of stores acquired overnight.

Phase 3 — Needs 6mo data
📊 Aggregated market data — FMCG brands

Real-time anonymized sales data from thousands of sari-sari stores. Sold to Unilever, Nestlé, San Miguel, distributors. No one in PH has this.

Phase 3–4 — Needs 10k+ stores
🛡️ Micro-insurance referrals

Partner with AXA, Igloo, or Sun Life. One-tap fire + inventory insurance inside the app. Referral commission per policy. Zero risk to Litro.
